OBJ
Week 3 Objectives
What students will be able to do by Friday
  • Use Nmap to discover hosts and scan for open ports on the local network
  • Use Wireshark to capture and read live traffic between the PC and the Pi
  • Identify what is being sent across the network and on which ports
  • Produce a Network Scan Report documenting their findings
D1
Day 1 — Who's on the Network?
Understand the two-device setup and run a first Nmap scan
Full Plan
80-minute session breakdown
▾
Intro 15 min
  • Introduce the lab: a PC and a Raspberry Pi connected on an air-gapped network
  • Draw on the board: [PC] ←→ [Raspberry Pi]
  • Review: What is an IP address? Run ip a on each device to find their addresses
Instructor Demo 20 min
  • Ping the Pi from the PC to confirm the connection: ping 192.168.x.x
  • Run a host discovery scan: nmap -sn 192.168.x.0/24
  • Show what a live host response looks like vs. no response
Scanner Pair Practice 35 min
  • Run ping from the PC to the Pi
  • Run nmap -sn 192.168.x.0/24 and record the results
  • Answer: How many hosts did you find? What are their IP addresses?
  • Writer pair begins documenting findings while Scanner pair runs tools
Wrap-Up 10 min
  • Teams share their host counts and IPs
  • Discuss: What does it mean that this network is air-gapped?
D2
Day 2 — What Ports Are Open?
Use Nmap to find open ports on both devices
Full Plan
80-minute session breakdown
▾
Review 10 min
  • Recap: We found two hosts. Today we look deeper — what services are running?
  • Analogy: Ports are like doors on a building. Open ports = unlocked doors.
Instructor Demo 20 min
  • Run a port scan against the Pi: nmap 192.168.x.x
  • Walk through the output: port number, state (open/closed), service name
  • Repeat the same scan against the PC — compare results
Scanner Pair Practice 40 min
  • Scan both devices and record every open port found
  • Fill in the Port Scan Table (see Deliverables section)
  • Writer pair drafts notes while scan runs
  • Answer: Which device has more open ports? Why might that be?
Wrap-Up 10 min
  • Teams compare port counts
  • Preview Day 3: Now let's watch the actual traffic moving across the wire
D3
Day 3 — Watching Traffic with Wireshark
Capture live packets between the PC and the Pi
Full Plan
80-minute session breakdown
▾
Intro to Wireshark 20 min
  • Instructor opens Wireshark and explains the three key panels:
  • Interface list — pick the right network adapter
  • Packet list — one row per packet
  • Packet details — what's inside each packet
  • Start a capture, ping the Pi, stop the capture — show the ping packets
Instructor Demo 15 min
  • Filter by IP: ip.addr == 192.168.x.x
  • Point out: source, destination, protocol, length
  • Click a single packet and explain what each field means
Scanner Pair Practice 35 min
  • Start a Wireshark capture on the PC
  • Ping the Pi while capturing
  • Stop the capture and locate the ICMP (ping) packets
  • Answer: What protocol did ping use? What does the data look like?
  • Writer pair labels the screenshot for the report
Wrap-Up 10 min
  • Share observations as a class
  • Discuss: Why would someone who shouldn't be on a network want to capture traffic?
D4
Day 4 — Scan & Capture Together
Run Nmap while Wireshark is capturing and compare results
Full Plan
80-minute session breakdown
▾
Setup 10 min
  • Scanner pair opens Wireshark and starts a capture on the PC
  • Keep Wireshark running in the background before scanning
Scan While Capturing 40 min
  • Run nmap 192.168.x.x while Wireshark is still capturing
  • Stop the capture after the scan finishes
  • Filter by the Pi's IP address in Wireshark
  • Answer: Can you see the Nmap scan packets? What do they look like?
  • Compare: How is scan traffic different from a regular ping?
Document Findings 20 min
  • Update the Port Scan Table with any new information
  • Writer pair adds a section to the report: "When we ran a scan, Wireshark showed…"
Wrap-Up 10 min
  • Class discussion: What are we actually seeing when we scan a network?
D5
Day 5 — Report & Reflection
Finalize findings and submit the week's report
Full Plan
80-minute session breakdown
▾
Finish Report 30 min
  • Complete the Port Scan Table for both devices
  • Write 2–3 sentences describing what the Wireshark capture showed
  • Answer the reflection questions below
Share Out 30 min
  • Each team shares one finding — a port, a packet, or something unexpected
  • Instructor wrap-up: "You just did what network analysts do. Scan, capture, document."
Preview Week 4 10 min
  • We now know what's running and what traffic looks like. Next week: What could go wrong?
Individual Reflection 10 min

Students write 3–5 sentences responding to these questions:

What did nmap tell you that ping couldn't?
What surprised you when you looked at traffic in Wireshark?
Why does it matter to know what ports are open on a device?

Quick-reference for all commands used this week

ip a

Find live hosts on the network

Find open ports on a host
nmap 192.168.x.x
Wireshark
Capture and read live network traffic
GUI → interface → Start

Filter traffic by IP address
