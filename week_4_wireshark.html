Wireshark Traffic Analysis

Two devices are sending data across the Auralab network. Your job is to open Wireshark, watch the traffic, and figure out what those devices are doing. You are not hacking anything — you are just watching. That is what network analysts do.

Network: Auralab
Tool: Wireshark
Teams: Ghost · Shadow · Fantom · Cipher
Roles: Driver · Navigator (swap every 10 min)
1
Join the Auralab Network
PASSWD Use the Password List handout from your previous session to find the Auralab password.
  1. Click the network icon in the top-right corner of the Kali desktop.
  2. Find Auralab in the list and click it.
  3. Type the password from your handout and press Connect.
  4. Wait until the icon shows signal bars — you are connected.
HINT Network not showing up? Click the icon again, wait 10 seconds, and look again.
ItemTeam Answer
Did you connect to Auralab?
2
Open Wireshark

Wireshark watches all the data moving across the network. Think of it like a security camera — but for internet traffic.

  1. Click Applications in the top-left corner.
  2. Click Sniffing & Spoofing.
  3. Click Wireshark.
HINT Wireshark not there? Open the Terminal and type wireshark — then press Enter.
3
Start the Capture

When Wireshark opens, you will see a list of network connections. You need to pick the right one.

  1. Look for wlan0 or wlan0mon in the list.
  2. Pick the one that has a wavy line moving next to it — that means traffic is flowing.
  3. Double-click it to start capturing.

Packets will start scrolling across the screen. That is normal. Just watch for now.

HINT Do not click anything in Wireshark yet. Just let it run for 30 seconds and watch what appears.
ItemTeam Answer
Which interface did you pick? (wlan0 or wlan0mon)
Are packets scrolling? (Yes / No)
4
Find the Two Devices

Look at the Source and Destination columns. Those are IP addresses — the names of devices on the network. Your job: find two IP addresses that keep showing up.

HINT Ignore anything that ends in .255 or starts with 224. — those are not real devices.
ItemTeam Answer
Device 1 IP Address
Device 2 IP Address
5
What Are They Sending?

Look at the Protocol column. That tells you what kind of data is being sent. Use the cheat sheet below.

ProtocolPlain English
ARP A device asking: who else is on this network?
HTTP Web page data — sometimes readable as plain text
TCP Two devices having a back-and-forth conversation
UDP One device sending data without waiting for a reply
ICMP A device pinging another to see if it is alive
ItemTeam Answer
What protocol do you see the most?
What do you think Device 1 is doing?
What do you think Device 2 is doing?
6
Wrap Up

Press the red square button at the top of Wireshark to stop the capture. Talk with your team, then write your answers.

QuestionTeam Answer
What was the most interesting thing you saw?
Could someone use this tool for bad reasons? How?
What is one question you still have?
// Turn In

Show your teacher this completed sheet with all six steps filled in.

  • Auralab connection confirmed
  • Interface selected
  • Two device IPs identified
  • Traffic type recorded
  • Reflection answers written
// Bonus — If You Finish Early
  • In the filter bar at the top, type arp then press Enter. How many ARP packets do you see?
  • Click on any one packet. Look at the bottom panel. Can you read any words in the right-side column?
